Discovering Lily of the Valley

Mark Gabriel Susim  |  May 15, 2026

These delicate bell-shaped flowers are known for their enchanting fragrance and have been celebrated in folklore, literature, and royal traditions for centuries. Despite their delicate appearance, they're surprisingly hardy plants that can thrive in shaded garden areas.

In this blog, I share my journey learning about this beautiful plant — from its botanical characteristics and cultural significance to how you can grow it in your own garden. Whether you're a beginner gardener or a flower enthusiast, there's something here for everyone.

The Lily of the Valley (Convallaria majalis) blooms in late spring, filling the air with one of the most cherished fragrances in the plant kingdom. Its nodding white bells hang in perfect rows along a slender arching stem — a sight that has inspired artists, poets, and perfumers for centuries.

Symbolism & Cultural Significance

Mark Gabriel Susim  |  May 10, 2026

Lily of the Valley carries rich symbolism across different cultures. It is traditionally associated with humility, purity, and the return of happiness. In Christian legend, the flowers sprang from the tears of the Virgin Mary — earning the name "Our Lady's Tears."

In France, Lily of the Valley (muguet) is gifted as a token of good luck on May 1st. This tradition dates back to 1561 when King Charles IX received a sprig as a lucky charm and decided to offer them annually to the ladies of his court. Today, approximately 60 million stems are sold across France on that single day.

The flower also holds royal significance — featured in Queen Elizabeth II's 1953 coronation bouquet, Kate Middleton's 2011 wedding flowers, and Princess Grace of Monaco's 1956 bridal arrangement.

Growing & Caring Tips

Mark Gabriel Susim  |  May 5, 2026

Lily of the Valley is one of the most rewarding woodland plants to grow. Here are some essential tips for success:

  • Planting Time: Best planted in fall, but can also go in during early spring
  • Light: Prefers partial to full shade — ideal under trees or along north-facing walls
  • Soil: Moist, well-drained, humus-rich soil; slightly acidic pH 6.0–7.0
  • Watering: Keep consistently moist but never waterlogged
  • Spacing: Plant rhizomes (pips) about 6 inches apart
  • Propagation: Divide rhizomes in fall or early spring every 4–5 years

⚠️ Toxicity Warning

All parts of this plant are highly toxic to humans, dogs, and cats. Keep children and pets away. If ingested, contact poison control immediately.
