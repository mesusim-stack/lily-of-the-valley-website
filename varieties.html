Lily of the Valley Varieties

10 cultivars of Convallaria majalis — from classic white to pink, striped, and golden

About the Cultivars

While Convallaria majalis is a single species, centuries of cultivation have produced a wide range of named cultivars. These vary in flower size, colour, leaf markings, fragrance intensity, and growth habit. Below are the 10 most significant and widely grown cultivars.

Perennial Zones 2–9 Shade Lover All Parts Toxic

Convallaria majalis

The Classic Species

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
6–8 in Zones 2–9 White Bells

The original species, beloved for centuries. Produces slender arching stems (racemes) carrying 5–13 nodding white bells from April to May. Forms dense colonies via underground rhizomes. Perfect as a woodland groundcover under deciduous trees. The benchmark against which all cultivars are measured.

Key traits: Classic shape, strong sweet scent, excellent groundcover, naturalises readily.

Fortin's Giant

Larger, More Dramatic Blooms

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
10–14 in Zones 3–9 Large White

One of the most popular cultivars for cut flowers. Produces noticeably larger, rounder bells on stronger stems, up to 14 inches tall. The fragrance is particularly powerful and long-lasting. Flowers slightly later than the species, extending the season. Widely used in florist bouquets and wedding arrangements.

Key traits: Tall stems, large bells, outstanding fragrance, excellent for cutting.

Rosea

The Pink Bell

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐
6–8 in Zones 3–8 Pink Bells

A rare and sought-after cultivar producing blush-pink to mauve-pink bell-shaped flowers instead of the usual white. The pink colour is most pronounced when the buds first open, fading slightly as the flowers age. A collector's treasure and a wonderful conversation piece in any garden.

Key traits: Unique pink flowers, slightly less vigorous than species, best in dappled shade.

Albostriata

Cream-Striped Foliage

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
6–8 in Zones 3–8 Variegated

Grown as much for its striking foliage as its flowers. The broad lance-shaped leaves are decorated with bold cream or pale yellow longitudinal stripes. The classic white bells appear above the variegated leaves in spring, creating a two-season display of interest. Excellent for brightening dark corners of a shaded garden.

Key traits: Ornamental striped leaves, white flowers, two-season interest.

Flore Pleno

Double Flowers

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐
6–8 in Zones 3–8 Double White

A charming cultivar with fully double white flowers — each bell packed with extra petals, giving a rosette-like appearance. The double flowers are slightly heavier and more rounded than the single species, and they last a little longer on the stem. A distinctive and elegant variation that adds textural interest to any planting.

Key traits: Double rosette-like flowers, slightly longer vase life, refined appearance.

Prolificans

Branching Stems

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐
8–10 in Zones 3–8 Clustered

An unusual cultivar with distinctly branched flower stems — unlike any other Lily of the Valley. Instead of a single arching raceme, Prolificans produces multiple branching clusters of small, tightly packed bells. The effect is dense and somewhat wild. A true botanical curiosity and a favourite among collectors seeking something out of the ordinary.

Key traits: Branching stems, clustered small bells, unusual wild appearance.

Reginae

Queen of Fragrance

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
8–10 in Zones 3–9 Strongest Scent

Named for its royal fragrance — reginae is Latin for "of the queen." Considered by many growers to have the most powerful and lingering scent of any Lily of the Valley cultivar. The flowers are pure white, slightly larger than the species, on sturdy erect stems. A single vase can fill an entire room with its intoxicating perfume.

Key traits: Exceptionally powerful fragrance, sturdy stems, pure white bells.

Hardwick Hall

Bold Leaf Margins

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
8–10 in Zones 3–8 Margined Leaves

Distinguished by its broad, bold leaves edged with a conspicuous creamy-yellow or pale green margin — named after Hardwick Hall in Derbyshire, England, where it was discovered growing in the estate garden. The white flowers are of the classic form. An elegant and architectural plant that provides year-round leaf interest in a shaded border.

Key traits: Broad leaves with pale margins, architectural quality, four-season interest.

Fernwood's Golden Slippers

Golden Foliage

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐
6–8 in Zones 3–8 Gold Leaves

A striking and relatively recent introduction with warm golden-yellow foliage that glows in the shade garden. The contrast between the luminous golden leaves and the classic white bells is breathtaking. The golden colour is most intense in spring when new growth emerges, gradually softening to chartreuse in summer. Thrives in part shade — full sun will scorch the leaves.

Key traits: Glowing golden-yellow leaves, white bells, vivid spring colour.

Green Tapestry

Vigorous Groundcover

Fragrance: ⭐⭐⭐⭐
6–8 in Zones 2–9 Groundcover

Selected specifically for its vigorous spreading habit and rich, deep-green foliage. Green Tapestry forms a dense, weed-suppressing carpet that thrives even in deep shade where most plants struggle. The classic white fragrant flowers appear each spring above glossy foliage. Ideal for filling large shaded areas under established trees or on difficult north-facing slopes.

Key traits: Vigorous spreader, deep green foliage, excellent weed suppressor, tough and reliable.

Quick Comparison

  • Tallest: Fortin's Giant (14 in)
  • Best fragrance: Reginae
  • Pink flowers: Rosea
  • Double flowers: Flore Pleno
  • Striped leaves: Albostriata
  • Gold leaves: Fernwood's Golden Slippers
  • Bold margins: Hardwick Hall
  • Groundcover: Green Tapestry
  • Most unusual: Prolificans

Buying Tips

  • Buy rhizomes ("pips") in autumn or spring
  • Choose plump, firm rhizomes with a visible growing tip
  • Plant immediately — do not let pips dry out
  • Available from specialist bulb suppliers online
  • Rare cultivars (Rosea, Fernwood's) may need a specialist nursery

Safety Reminder

All 10 cultivars are equally toxic. Every part of the plant — including the vase water — is dangerous. Keep away from children and pets.
