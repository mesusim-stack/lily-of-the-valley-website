Frequently Asked Questions

Can I grow Lily of the Valley in a container?

Yes! Plant rhizomes in a deep container with well-draining, humus-rich compost. Keep in partial shade and water consistently. The plant may need repotting or dividing every 2–3 years.

My pet may have eaten some — what should I do?

Contact a vet or animal poison control immediately. All parts of Lily of the Valley are toxic to dogs and cats. Do not wait for symptoms — act straight away.

Can I submit my own photos?

Yes! Send your images via the contact form above (select "Share a Photo"). Include a description of the variety and where it was taken. We may feature it on the blog.

Where can I buy Lily of the Valley rhizomes?

They are widely available from garden centres in autumn and spring, and from online bulb specialists. Look for named cultivars like Fortin's Giant or Rosea for something special.
